# Day 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity

## 🎯 Learning Objectives
Understand the definition of cybersecurity and the core frameworks that guide the industry.

## 🧠 Key Concepts
### What is Cybersecurity?
The practice of protecting computer systems, networks, devices, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access, damage, or theft.

### Job Roles
* **SOC Analyst:** Monitors network traffic and security alerts.
* **Penetration Tester:** Simulates attacks (White/Black/Grey hat).
* **Digital Forensics:** Investigates incidents and collects evidence.
* **Security Engineer:** Builds/secures systems, firewalls, and IDS.
* **Red Teamer:** Simulates real-world attacks to identify defensive gaps.

### The CIA Triad
A conceptual framework for protecting data:
1. **Confidentiality:** Only authorized access (Encryption, Access Control).
2. **Integrity:** Ensuring data isn't tampered with (Hashing, Digital Signatures).
3. **Availability:** Systems must be running when needed (DDoS protection, Backups).

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
The 5 Core Functions:
1. **IDENTIFY:** Know your assets.
2. **PROTECT:** Safeguard them (e.g., Firewalls).
3. **DETECT:** Spot when something is off (e.g., SIEM, IDS).
4. **RESPOND:** Contain the damage.
5. **RECOVER:** Get back to normal.
